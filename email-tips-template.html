Fitness Tips & Tricks

Level Up Your Fitness Game 🎯

Ready to take your training to the next level? These are the strategies I use with my top-performing clients—and now they're yours.

1

Master Your Protein Timing

Want to maximize muscle recovery and keep hunger at bay? Aim for 25-35g of protein within 30-60 minutes after your workout. Your muscles are like sponges post-training—this is when they absorb nutrients most efficiently.

PRO TIP:

Keep a protein shake or Greek yogurt in your gym bag. Quick, convenient, and hits your macros perfectly.

2

Use the 80/20 Rule for Calories

Perfection is the enemy of progress. If 80% of your meals hit your macro targets, you're winning. That other 20%? Room for life, celebrations, and flexibility without derailing your goals.

PRO TIP:

Track your meals in the app for 5 days this week. You'll be amazed how much awareness alone improves your choices.

3

Progressive Overload is Key

Your body adapts to the stress you put on it. To keep building strength, you need to progressively increase the challenge. This doesn't always mean more weight—it could be more reps, slower tempo, or shorter rest periods.

PRO TIP:

Each week, aim to add 1-2 reps to your working sets OR add 5-10 lbs to the bar. Small increases compound into massive gains.

4

Hydration Affects Everything

Even 2% dehydration can tank your performance, energy, and recovery. Aim for half your body weight in ounces daily (e.g., 150 lbs = 75 oz of water). Add more if you're training hard or it's hot.

PRO TIP:

Start your day with 16-20 oz of water before coffee. It kickstarts your metabolism and rehydrates you after sleep.

5

Recovery is Where Growth Happens

You don't build muscle in the gym—you build it during recovery. Prioritize 7-9 hours of sleep, take rest days seriously, and incorporate active recovery like walking or light stretching.

PRO TIP:

If you're sore for more than 72 hours, you went too hard. Scale back intensity and focus on form over ego.

These aren't just tips—they're the fundamentals that separate people who see results from people who spin their wheels. Implement even ONE of these this week and watch what happens.
